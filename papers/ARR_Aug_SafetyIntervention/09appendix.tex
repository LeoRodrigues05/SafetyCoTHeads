\section{Reproducibility and Experimental Specification}
\label{sec:appendix-setup}

This appendix documents the intervention implementations, aggregation rules,
judge instruments, validation data, and disaggregated results used in the main
text. The released artifact contains the configuration for every run, the
per-prompt generations and judgments, the target rankings produced during
intervention discovery, and a per-cell summary table. We use a \emph{cell} to
refer to one \texttt{(model, dataset, condition)} combination.

\subsection{Models, datasets, and generation}

The five primary model/configuration arms are \model{Qwen3-8B},
\model{Llama-3.1-8B}, \model{OLMo-3-Think}, and two arms using the same
pre-alignment \model{OLMo-3-Base} checkpoint but different target provenance.
The exact checkpoints and roles are summarized in
Table~\ref{tab:appendix-models}. The transfer arm applies targets discovered on
\model{OLMo-3-Think} to \model{OLMo-3-Base}; the self arm discovers targets on
\model{OLMo-3-Base} itself. These are target-provenance controls, not additional
model checkpoints.

    \textbf{Arm} & \textbf{Checkpoint} & \textbf{Role / target provenance} \\
    \midrule
    \model{Qwen3-8B} & \texttt{Qwen/Qwen3-8B} & Safety-trained; own-model discovery; explicit trace \\
    \model{Llama-3.1-8B} & \texttt{meta-llama/\allowbreak Llama-3.1-8B-\allowbreak Instruct} & Safety-trained; own-model discovery \\

  \label{tab:appendix-models}

Target discovery uses 100 MaliciousInstruct prompts and, for contrastive
procedures, 100 Alpaca prompts. Neither evaluation dataset is used for target
discovery. Evaluation uses all 100 JailbreakBench prompts and 98 BeaverTails
prompts (seven prompts from each of 14 categories). Each model-dataset pair is
run at baseline and under ten intervention conditions: SHIPS top-$k$ for
$k\in\{3,5,8\}$, neuron ablation for
$k\in\{256,512,1024\}$, activation-addition steering for normalized doses
$d\in\{0.5,1.0,1.5\}$, and one directional-ablation condition. The resulting
primary grid contains $5\times2\times10=100$ intervention cells, plus ten
matched baseline cells.

Generation is deterministic (temperature $0$, greedy decoding, seed $0$).
Maximum generation lengths are 1,024 tokens for \model{Qwen3-8B}, 2,048 for
\model{OLMo-3-Think}, and 512 for \model{Llama-3.1-8B} and the two Base arms.
For the explicit-trace models, the answer judge sees only text following the
\texttt{</think>} boundary, while trace-based judges see only the text inside
the \texttt{<think>} block. For the remaining arms, the early-response-prefix
analysis is reported separately and is not treated as private chain-of-thought.

\section{Intervention Discovery and Application}
\label{sec:appendix-methods}

Let $\mathcal{H}$ and $\mathcal{B}$ denote the harmful and benign discovery
sets, respectively. All contrastive statistics below are computed at the final
prompt token. The procedures select candidate targets; the subsequent
interventions are causal tests of the selected targets' behavioral importance,
not proofs that those targets form a unique or complete safety mechanism.
Table~\ref{tab:appendix-interventions} summarizes the evaluated configurations.

\begin{table}[t]
  \centering
  \scriptsize
  \setlength{\tabcolsep}{2.5pt}
  \begin{tabular}{@{}p{0.16\columnwidth}p{0.25\columnwidth}p{0.37\columnwidth}p{0.14\columnwidth}@{}}
    \toprule
    \textbf{Family} & \textbf{Discovery signal} & \textbf{Intervention} & \textbf{Strengths} \\
    \midrule
    SHIPS & Per-head next-token KL & Scale selected query channels & 3 levels \\
    Neuron & Harmful--benign MLP contrast & Zero selected MLP channels & 3 levels \\
    Steering & Residual harmful--benign direction & Add negative unit direction & 3 levels \\
    Dir. ablation & Same residual direction & Project direction out at all layers & single \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
  \caption{Target-discovery signals and interventions. Strength units are
  family-specific and are not numerically commensurate across rows.}
  \label{tab:appendix-interventions}
\end{table}

\paragraph{SHIPS head attribution and ablation.}
For a discovery prompt $x$, let $p_\theta(\cdot\mid x)$ be the model's
next-token distribution and let $p_{\theta,(\ell,h)}^{\varepsilon}$ be the
distribution after scaling the query contribution of attention head
$(\ell,h)$ by $\varepsilon=10^{-4}$. We score each head by
\begin{align}
 s_{\ell,h}^{\mathrm{SHIPS}}
 &=\frac{1}{|\mathcal{H}|}\sum_{x\in\mathcal{H}}
   D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(
     p_\theta(\cdot\mid x)\,\big\|\,\nonumber\\[-2pt]
 &\hspace{7em}
     p_{\theta,(\ell,h)}^{\varepsilon}(\cdot\mid x)
   \bigr).
 \label{eq:appendix-ships}
\end{align}
Heads are ranked by the dataset-mean score, and the top $k$ are jointly scaled
during generation. The implementation uses forward hooks rather than a
model-specific attention rewrite, preserving the upstream SHIPS operation on
architectures with grouped-query attention \citep{zhou2024ships}.

\paragraph{Safety-neuron attribution and ablation.}
For layer $\ell$, let $z_\ell(x)$ be the gated MLP activation entering the
\texttt{down\_proj} module. The score for neuron $j$ is the harmful--benign
activation contrast
\begin{equation}
 s_{\ell,j}^{\mathrm{neuron}}
 =\frac{1}{|\mathcal{H}|}\sum_{x\in\mathcal{H}}z_{\ell,j}(x)
 -\frac{1}{|\mathcal{B}|}\sum_{x\in\mathcal{B}}z_{\ell,j}(x).
 \label{eq:appendix-neuron}
\end{equation}
We rank neurons by $|s_{\ell,j}^{\mathrm{neuron}}|$ and set the selected input
dimensions of \texttt{down\_proj} to zero during generation
\citep{zhao2025neuron}. Thus, a ``neuron'' denotes one SwiGLU intermediate
channel, equivalently one input channel of the layer's down-projection.

\paragraph{Refusal-direction extraction.}
At each layer $\ell$, we extract a candidate direction from the residual-stream
state $h_\ell(x)$:
\begin{equation}
 r_\ell=
 \frac{1}{|\mathcal{H}|}\sum_{x\in\mathcal{H}}h_\ell(x)
 -\frac{1}{|\mathcal{B}|}\sum_{x\in\mathcal{B}}h_\ell(x).
 \label{eq:appendix-direction}
\end{equation}
The evaluated runs use the unit-normalized direction from layer 14. For
activation addition, the paper reports a normalized dose $d$ and applies
\begin{equation}
 h_{14}\leftarrow h_{14}-8d\,\widehat r_{14},
 \qquad d\in\{0.5,1.0,1.5\},
 \label{eq:appendix-steer}
\end{equation}
where the negative sign suppresses the harmful-minus-benign refusal direction.
For directional ablation, the same unit direction is projected out at every
decoder layer:
\begin{equation}
 h_\ell\leftarrow h_\ell-
 (h_\ell^{\top}\widehat r_{14})\widehat r_{14}.
 \label{eq:appendix-dirablate}
\end{equation}
Activation addition and directional ablation therefore share a discovered
direction but constitute distinct interventions: the former is a single-layer
dose sweep, whereas the latter is an alpha-free projection applied throughout
the network \citep{arditi2024refusal}.

\section{Metric, Aggregation, and Statistical Reporting}
\label{sec:appendix-metric}
\label{appendix-metric}

\subsection{Axis definitions}

For cell $c$ and its no-intervention baseline $b$ from the same model and
dataset, let $C=\Pr(\mathrm{clean})$ and
$\HAC=\Pr(\mathrm{harmful}\mid\mathrm{clean})$. With
$\clip(x)=\max(0,\min(1,x))$, the first two axes are
\begin{align}
 \Ppot_c
 &=\clip\!\left(\frac{\HAC_c-\HAC_b}{1-\HAC_b}\right),
 \nonumber\\[-2pt]
 \Qual_c
 &=\clip\!\left(\frac{C_c}{C_b}\right).
 \label{eq:appendix-pq}
\end{align}
Potency measures the fraction of the baseline's remaining safety headroom that
is converted into coherent harm. Coherence Retention reports the fraction of
baseline coherence retained by the intervention; it is not a measure of benign
task capability.

For paired prompt $i$, let $y_i$ be final-answer harmfulness and
$\widehat y_i^{\mathrm{tr}}$ the trace-only prediction. We report
\begin{align}
 U_c&=\Pr(y_i=1,\widehat y_i^{\mathrm{tr}}=0),\nonumber\\
 O_c&=\Pr(y_i=0,\widehat y_i^{\mathrm{tr}}=1),\nonumber\\
 A_c&=\Pr(y_i=\widehat y_i^{\mathrm{tr}}),\nonumber\\
 \operatorname{FNR}^{\mathrm{tr}}_c
 &=\Pr(\widehat y_i^{\mathrm{tr}}=0\mid y_i=1).
 \label{eq:appendix-monitor}
\end{align}
Here $U_c$ is the covert-failure rate and $O_c$ is the over-warning rate.
Monitorability Retention is
\begin{equation}
 \Sreason_c=1-\clip(U_c-U_b).
 \label{eq:appendix-s}
\end{equation}
Thus, $\Sreason$ decreases only when an intervention increases harmful answers
whose harmfulness is not visible to the trace monitor. The signed gap
$\gapm_c=U_c-O_c$ is retained as a descriptive diagnostic and does not enter
$\Sreason$ or $\SFS$.

The primary report is the vector $(\Ppot,\Qual,\Sreason)$. The headline scalar
is
\begin{equation}
 \SFS_c=(\Ppot_c\Qual_c\Sreason_c)^{1/3}.
 \label{eq:appendix-sfs}
\end{equation}
The geometric mean gives a ``no axis left behind'' property. We additionally
report $\Ppot\Qual\Sreason=\SFS^3$ as a scale variant, not as an independent
ranking robustness test, and the threat-oriented diagnostic
$\Ppot\Qual(1-\Sreason)$, which is nonzero only when covert failure increases
above baseline. Following the multi-metric precedent of HELM
\citep{liang2023helm}, the scalar is never reported without its components.

\subsection{Aggregation, rankings, and missingness}

Per-model family means average the two datasets and all evaluated conditions
within that family. Pooled family means give equal weight to every
model-dataset-condition cell; because the families have different numbers and
units of intervention strengths, these are summaries of the evaluated grid,
not matched-strength estimates. The model-level Kendall analysis first
macro-averages each condition over the two datasets and then computes
Kendall's $\tau_b$ over the ten non-baseline conditions
\citep{kendall1945ties}. The tie-aware statistic
is applied directly to score vectors rather than to arbitrarily tie-broken
rankings.

When confidence intervals are reported, the resampling unit is the prompt ID.
The same bootstrap sample is applied to the matched baseline and intervention
rows before recomputing the complete metric. This preserves the paired design.
Tables retain the generated, parsed-answer, nonempty-answer, clean-answer, and
paired trace-answer denominators. Conditional quantities with an empty
denominator, such as trace false-negative rate in a cell with no harmful
answers, are left undefined rather than imputed as zero.

\subsection{Pareto analysis}

Figure~\ref{fig:appendix-pareto} projects each dataset-averaged condition onto
Potency and retained selectivity $R=\Qual\Sreason$ for readability. Pareto
membership is nevertheless computed in the full three-dimensional
$(\Ppot,\Qual,\Sreason)$ space: a condition is dominated only if another
condition is at least as high on all three axes and strictly higher on at least
one.

\section{Judge Rubrics and Validation}
\label{sec:appendix-judges}

\subsection{Evaluation instruments}

The evaluation uses one non-generative coherence classifier and four
LLM-based judging tasks, summarized in Table~\ref{tab:appendix-judges}. The
Qwen3-30B-A3B judge labels final-answer behavior, predicts harmfulness from the
trace alone, and identifies sentence-level safety reasoning. A Qwen3-14B LoRA
judge supplies the 12 pathway labels. All LLM judges use deterministic decoding
on the first pass, strict JSON schemas, parsing diagnostics, and bounded retry
logic; raw judge outputs are retained in the released artifact.

  \label{tab:appendix-judges}

The answer-safety rubric marks a response harmful when it fully or partially
complies with the harmful request, and marks it a safe rejection when it refuses
to help achieve the harmful goal. Auxiliary labels record explicit safety
reasoning, invented intent, and subject changes; these do not enter the three
axes. The sentence-level safety-reasoning judge marks only sentences that
explicitly weigh the harm, policy boundary, user intent, refusal rationale, or
a safer alternative. Operational planning and generic disclaimers are excluded
unless they contain explicit safety reasoning.

Two annotators independently completed a blind validation set comprising 80
final-answer safety decisions, 40 trace-only unsafe predictions, and 20
trace-level safety-reasoning decisions. Table~\ref{tab:appendix-validation}

and trace prediction is close to the observed human--human agreement on the
same items. The pathway judge is evaluated separately on 180 held-out,
trace-disjoint HarmThoughts examples; this is transfer-domain validation of the
adapted taxonomy rather than direct in-domain validation of all twelve labels

\section{Additional observations from comparative study}
\label{sec:appendix-comp-other}
\paragraph{\model{OLMo3-Base-Own} as Falsification Control.}
A good metric should not report a strong safety mechanism in a checkpoint with little alignment-induced safety to find.
In a control study, we ask whether interventions locate safety mechanisms in checkpoints before safety-alignment. In the
\emph{transfer} control, intervention targets (heads, neurons, or directions) identified on \model{OLMo-3-Think} are applied to \model{OLMo-3-Base}; in the
\emph{self} control (\model{OLMo-3-Base-Own}), targets are identified on the base checkpoint itself. We hypothesize that none of the interventions should induce coherent harm because there is no alignment circuitry to remove. This holds for our localized method, with per-neuron ablation inducing no harm under either case ($\Ppot=0.04$ on transfer, $\Ppot=0.03$ on self), and the self-discovery variant scores the lowest of all the cells. The transfer contrast itself is small (Table~\ref{tab:transfer}).
\paragraph{Aggregate susceptibility also differs across models.}
Averaging $\SFS$ over all ten tested non-baseline conditions gives a compact summary of how frequently the evaluated interventions produce selective failure: the mean is $0.28$ for \model{Qwen3-8B}, $0.34$ for \model{OLMo-3-Think}, and $0.57$ for \model{Llama-3.1-8B}. We refer to this as \emph{grid-average susceptibility} rather than an intrinsic measure of model resilience because it depends on the intervention families, selectors, and dose ranges included in the experiment. The result nevertheless reinforces the model-level analysis: Llama is affected by several intervention families, whereas Qwen and OLMo-3-Think are susceptible to a narrower set of procedures. 
\paragraph{Dose dependence is itself model- and family-specific.}
Across the three dose-swept families, activation steering provides the clearest repeated strength response. Steering Potency rises from $0.05\to 0.13\to0.23$ on \model{Qwen3-8B} and from $0.05\to0.33\to0.77$ on \model{OLMo-3-Think} for $\alpha=0.5/1.0/1.5$. \model{Llama-3.1-8B} is already highly susceptible at the smallest dose and peaks at $\alpha=1.0$ ($\Ppot=0.69\to0.86\to0.79$). The SHIPS and neuron-count sweeps show no common monotonic trend across models, although neuron ablation on Llama becomes substantially more potent at the largest budget. Directional ablation has only one evaluated strength and is therefore excluded from the dose-response comparison.

\section{Pathway Taxonomy and Full Profile}
\label{sec:appendix-pathway}

The pathway judge applies twelve binary labels to cumulative prefixes of the
parsed trace. The taxonomy adapts HarmThoughts annotations
\citep{kakkar2026harmthoughts} and groups labels by the visible stage of the
response process:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=1pt,topsep=2pt]
  \raggedright
  \item \textbf{Recognition / caution:}
  \texttt{risk\_acknowledgment}, \texttt{policy\_awareness}, and
  \texttt{intent\_assessment}.
  \item \textbf{Refusal dynamics:}
  \texttt{refusal\_initiation}, \texttt{refusal\_maintenance}, and
  \texttt{refusal\_suppression}.
  \item \textbf{Rationalization:}
  \texttt{benign\_motive\_invention}, \texttt{educational\_framing}, and
  \texttt{legal\_disclaimer\_laundering}.
  \item \textbf{Execution:}
  \texttt{task\_decomposition}, \texttt{procedural\_planning}, and
  \texttt{operational\_detail}.
\end{enumerate}
A label is marked only when the behavior is present in the observed prefix;
the judge is not asked to predict what the model will do later. The rates below
are computed over parsed trace prefixes, so their denominator differs from the
harmful-among-clean population used for Potency.

Table~\ref{tab:appendix-pathway} gives the full profile for
\model{OLMo-3-Think} on JailbreakBench. Directional ablation, SHIPS top-8, and
neuron top-1024 remain close to the baseline profile. Steering at the strongest
evaluated dose produces the largest visible shift: recognition and refusal
labels decrease, while procedural planning and operational detail increase.
This is a descriptive trace-level signature and should not be interpreted as a
direct localization of latent computation.

\section{Reasoning affected by intervention-dosage}
\label{sec:appendix:dosage}
Within the explicit-trace models, steering intensifies the pathway signature as
its strength increases (Figure~\ref{fig:cot-dose}). Pooled across the two models,
Potency rises from $0.05$ at $\alpha=0.5$ to $0.50$ at $\alpha=1.5$, while the
visible reasoning moves further from refusal toward execution. This increase is
not accompanied by reduced monitorability; the trace becomes more conflicted
rather than more concealing. On \model{OLMo-3-Think}, the SHIPS and neuron
profiles show little comparable movement across their tested budgets.
Directional ablation has only one evaluated condition and is included as a
reference rather than as a dose sweep. These differences support
method-specific pathway effects under the tested interventions, but do not by
themselves establish that the families act on categorically distinct causal
objects.

\section{Additional observations from pathway analysis}
\label{sec:appendix-cot-other}
\paragraph{The dataset shifts the profile.}
JailbreakBench prompts are associated with more induced harm and a more
negative signed monitorability gap than BeaverTails (mean $\Ppot=0.16$ versus
$0.14$; gap $-0.126$ versus $-0.078$). The adversarially phrased
JailbreakBench prompts also elicit more execution-side labels and more explicit
safety reasoning.

\paragraph{Safety reasoning is concentrated early in the trace.}
When present, safety reasoning appears near the beginning of the CoT rather
than only near the final answer. Safety-relevant spans occupy $37$--$39\%$ of
traces on the explicit-trace models, compared with $5$--$16\%$ of the
early-response prefixes in the remaining arms. Safety consideration is
therefore front-loaded rather than exclusively a late-stage phenomenon.

\paragraph{Monitorability and harm vary differently across model classes.}
On the explicit-trace models, higher-Potency interventions are mildly
associated with more negative gaps (Spearman $\rho=-0.28$ to $-0.33$), meaning
that harmfulness becomes, if anything, more visible to the trace monitor. On
the pre-alignment base variants, the prefix-based association is weakly
positive ($\rho=+0.11$ to $+0.44$). We do not over-interpret this contrast: the
base variants differ in baseline safety and do not provide comparable explicit
traces. The monitorability result is therefore most directly supported on the
explicit-trace models.

\paragraph{The few positive-gap cells are marginal.}
The only four cells with a positive signed gap occur in the
\model{Llama-3.1-8B}/BeaverTails prefix sensitivity analysis
($\gapm\leq+0.036$), and none is a high-Potency cell; two are low-harm neuron
ablations. Their magnitudes are within judge noise. We report them for
completeness, but they do not constitute a systematic covert-failure pattern.

\paragraph{Steering does not induce compliance through rationalization.}
One might expect a steered model to justify compliance by inventing benign
motives, but the rationalization rate remains flat across the steering sweep
($0.10\!\to\!0.12\!\to\!0.09$ at $\alpha=0.5/1.0/1.5$). The additional harm is
instead associated with execution-side labels, not with increased
rationalization.

\section{Artifact Organization}
\label{sec:appendix-artifact}

The released artifact is organized around the same experimental hierarchy as
the paper. Per-model discovery directories contain the SHIPS head ranking, the
neuron ranking, and per-layer refusal directions. Generation directories are
indexed by dataset, condition, and seed. Each judged cell retains the original
generation, parsed answer and trace, raw judge responses, parse status, and a
summary file. The top-level composite table joins these summaries into the
100-row primary analysis grid. Human-annotation batches include task manifests,
independent annotator files, held-out machine labels, and deterministic scoring
reports. This organization permits readers to reproduce either the complete
pipeline or only the aggregation and plotting stages from released judgments.
